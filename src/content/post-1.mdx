The COVID-19 pandemic has severely impacted industries worldwide, including live events that have seen a decline in attendance. Stakeholders are seeking innovative solutions to expand the remote viewing experience, connecting fans with each other and athletes during TV shows.
We are grateful to [HypeSportsInnovation](https://www.hypesportsinnovation.com) for organizing [this event](https://www.hypesportsinnovation.com/HYPE-UniTrento-2021-Wintersports-Hackathon) in collaboration with the [University of Trento](https://www.unitn.it/en) to address this issue.

During the event, we had 36 hours to analyze data from sensors on skiers' boots and 
video sequences. Our challenge was to extract useful information, metrics, and comparisons
to enhance fan engagement.

The data we analyzed was from a race that took place on January 24th in KitzbÃ¼hel, Austria.

## Idea

Our team aimed to develop a mobile application that could engage spectators during live events.
We wanted to create a game that was simple and inclusive, which led us to the idea of using the "<span style="color:green"> _green light_ </span> / <span style="color:red"> _red light_ </span>" concept.
The game would sync with the descending athlete's live data, and a question would appear on the user's screen. The user would have the choice between pressing a green or red button, corresponding to the classic colors used on TV to indicate whether the skier is ahead or behind in the competition.
